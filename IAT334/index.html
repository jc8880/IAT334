OUR RESEARCH

DESIGN DOMAIN: A design to help with daily user welfare & wellness

  • “The distinction between work and everything else, already a blurry line for most Americans, got even blurrier” (Jenna Wortham, 2021)
  • Based on the existing market of wellness apps, calmness and sleep are very common goals that could be improved or solved
  • Notably, productivity and self-optimization are key motivations of those seeking improved wellness
  • For the older demographic, achieving productivity may feel increasingly important as we physically cannot do as much as we once did

Downloading a wellness app can assist in offering opportunities for users, specifically seniors, to become better involved in making progress towards a personal goal

Why do we need this application? What makes it different from our competitors?

Early Riser is designed to be easy to use and navigate for the elderly and older users. What makes this application unique and different from our competitor is that it promotes productivity with our unique feature called guided activities, which is a compilation of videos on relaxing activities that the users could use.

The application also offer flexible scheduling and also allow the user to customized their own to-do list. Futhermore through this application intermingling of these two feature, we hope to eliminate boredom and introduce the elderly users to the new activities that will help them start their day.

Instead of the simple to-do list application, our application specifically revolves around being as application that users would want to open as soon as they wake up.

Interaction Framework

Our Intended User

Base on our earlier research for this application design, our intended users would be seniors and older adults looking for ways to improve and maintain their ways of life as they get older.

This application would addresses their boredom and offers them something to look forward to each day

This application would also preserve their sense of personal productivity, especially as seniors begin to have less work/jobs and more free time “Wellness has become synonymous with productivity and self-optimization” (Wortham, 2021).

Finally, this application is meant to be elderly friendly to accommodate users who may not be as familiar with navigating technology and prevent senior frustrations.

Prototyping

User testing

Our Plan

Prepare what tasks the participants would do in the application.

Allow the participants to go through the application.

Prepare the usability testing questionnaire.

Interview the participants regarding the application.

Our Methods

We would allow the participants to use the application uninterrupted for the specified time or until they complete the task

We would take notes of how they were approaching the completion of our assigned tasks and any other usability problems

We also would be taking notes and help them when they are struggling with some tasks but otherwise allow the user to experience the application

Tasks

A few pre-determined tasks were chosen each time, for example:

“Add a reminder for ‘lunch’ to your daily reminders list”

Helps us determine if the main app feature is clear to understand

“Find and watch a video that most interests you”

Testing how the user navigates “guided activities” to find their personal interests

“Sign out of the app when you’re done for the day”

Interacting with an important, but less frequently occurring feature

Questions

DESIGN ISSUE 1: The progress bar featured at the bottom of the app’s main path is not clearly identified as one, it is too ambiguous

WHY?:

Mistaken interpretation, user mental model did not align with our intended perception

Our user attempted to click on it and hover around the area to see if anything additional occurred

commented on being unsure what it represented.

DESIGN ISSUE 2:

The time selector that indicates where on the timeline to place the reminder is too small for the users

WHY?:

The interaction requires more precision and control which may not be ideal for all users, especially seniors

Our user noted that if it was larger, they would feel better about having to use the feature

DESIGN ISSUE 3:

The lack of interaction and customization with each individual reminder entry

WHY?:

Users feel that it should be more personalized for them; the lack of customization is a concern towards catering to experienced audiences

The current interface of the reminders page cannot easily accommodate an extra section for user interaction and requires a redesign

Design consideration

DESIGN ISSUE 1 (progress bar):

The design of the progress bar too closely resembles the interactable buttons

To improve, the progress bar could use additional feedback such as text to indicate what it is progressing towards, clarifying its purpose to the user

DESIGN ISSUE 2 (Reminders time selector button):

Considering the context of use being on a mobile phone rather than desktop, we need to enlarge the text area to better match with the common surface area of a finger

DESIGN ISSUE 3 (Lack of Customization):

We should design a way for the user to add new entries to the list of activities they could choose from

To improve the functionality of the application, we should consider how we could allow these new entries to interact with the overall timeline of the to-do reminder page
